Scoring dimensions

Value score (1–5) — rooted in the five pillars (5 = highest value, 1 = lowest).

CriteriaWhat it means
Risk & Compliance ReductionHow much the project reduces safety, regulatory, or operational risk exposure.
Growth & ScalabilityContribution to capacity, future flexibility, or the ability to scale.
Operational EfficiencyExpected improvement to cost, cycle time, or process performance.
Facilities & Asset PerformanceImpact on the condition, reliability, or lifecycle value of physical assets and space.
Workplace Experience & CultureEffect on employee experience, satisfaction, or the culture the workplace supports.

Ease score (1–5) — how straightforward the project is to execute (5 = easiest, 1 = hardest).

CriteriaWhat it means
Resource & Budget AvailabilityWhether funding, staffing, and capital are already accessible or need to be secured.
Stakeholder & Approval ComplexityNumber of approvals, cross-functional dependencies, or competing priorities involved.
Data & System ReadinessWhether the data, systems, or infrastructure needed already exist and are reliable.
Change ReadinessHow much process change or behavior change is required from the teams affected.

Exception handling

An exception may be raised when one or more of the following is present:

  • A regulatory, legal, or contractual obligation with a hard deadline.
  • A safety or security risk that worsens the longer it is unaddressed.
  • A credible, time-bound risk.
  • A dependency blocking a disproportionate amount of downstream work.

Raising a request: Requests are submitted through the intake form and follow the normal review procedures. It must state the triggering condition, the deadline or risk, the work displaced, and the cost of inaction.
